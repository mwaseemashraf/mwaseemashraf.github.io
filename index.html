About Me

Hello! I'm M. Waseem Ashraf, a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Brigham Young University, working under the supervision of Dr. Eric R. Homer.

My research focuses on shape memory alloys, phase transformations, and the coupling between mechanical behavior and microstructural evolution. I use computational methods such as finite element modeling, crystal plasticity, and machine learning to better understand and predict material behavior.

I am also passionate about making research tools and computational methods accessible to the broader materials science community, and have contributed to several open-source modeling and simulation workflows.

Background

I grew up in Soon Valley, Pakistan, and earned my undergraduate degree in Mechanical Engineering from Pakistan Institute of Engineering and Applied Sciences (PIEAS) in 2019. I started my PhD at BYU in 2020.

I love to combine engineering rigor with computational innovation, and I aim to contribute to material design and discovery for energy-efficient and advanced applications.
