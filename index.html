About Me

Hi, I am Warda. I am a graduate student at the Usable Security and Privacy Group at the BYU Internet Research Lab. I am supervised by Dr. Daniel Zappala. I study technology, people, and their mutual interactions.

While I have my undergraduate level training mostly focused towards traditional computer science disciplines, such as mobile app development, studying computer networks, and implementing computer security algorithms, I have recently found my love for understanding how users interact with technology and vice versa. My current research area is ‘Usable’ Security and Privacy: the study of how to make cybersecurity and digital privacy more understandable and accessible to everyone, especially non-experts. To attain a truly secure cyberspace, addressing vulnerabilities that stem from human behaviors and choices also need to be considered in addition to addressing challenging scientific and engineering problems. I am very fascinated with socio-technical interactions and how people make their choices with technology.

My goal with this research is to help develop a more inclusive cyberspace so that *everyone* can be more secure and safe in the online world.

Journey

I belong from a small, beautiful village located in Soon Valley, Pakistan. In 2019, I got my undergraduate degree in Computer and Information Sciencs from Pakistan Institute of Engineering and Applied Sciences. In 2020, I started my PhD in Computer Science at Brigham Young University.

Being in Pakistan while applying to grad schools in the United States, I struggled with finding advice on how to go about the application process. If you are considering applying for CS grad schools in the United States, you are welcome to glance through the PhD Application Guide.
